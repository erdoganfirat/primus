                    ## Space-centric design
                    <p className="text-lg">Strategic spacing between UI elements directly impacts the relationships and hierarchy within the design.</p>

                    <div className="row row-cols-2">
                        <div className="col">
                            ### Relationship
                            <p>Elements positioned closely in a design are perceived as having meaningful relationships. As space increases between elements, their perceived connection diminishes.Spacing also fosters relationships, with elements arranged in the same pattern being seen as related pieces of equal importance. Space serves as a marker for associated information groups, enabling content sections on a page without requiring lines or graphical dividers.</p>
                        </div>
                        <div className="col">
                            <img src="spacing/relationship.png" srcset="spacing/relationship.png 1x, spacing/relationship@2x.png 2x" alt='Relationship'/>
                        </div>
                    </div>

                    <div className="row row-cols-2 reverse">
                        <div className="col">
                            ### Hierarchy
                            <p>The perception of importance is influenced by the amount of space surrounding elements. Elements placed closely together may be overlooked, as users tend to perceive them as a group rather than individual items. To ensure focus on elements of high importance, consider providing additional surrounding space to attract attention.</p>
                        </div>
                        <div className="col">
                            <img src="spacing/hierarchy.png" srcset="spacing/hierarchy.png 1x, spacing/hierarchy@2x.png 2x" alt='Hierarchy'/>
                        </div>
                    </div>

                    <div className="row row-cols-2">
                        <div className="col">
                            ### White space
                            <p>White space (empty space) is a crucial element in design. It serves multiple purposes, such as breaking up page sections and emphasizing specific elements. This gap aids information processing, preventing users from feeling overwhelmed by dense content. Although specific sections of the UI may contain dense content, it is essential to balance the overall page with white space to offer users a visual break and a more comfortable viewing experience.</p>

                    ### Small values
                    <p>Use values (0 to 8px) for small and compact elements.</p>
                    <strong className="text-md">EXAMPLES</strong>
                    <ul className="list disc mb32">
                        <li>Gap between small icons and text</li>
                        <li>Container padding of small components (ie badges, icon buttons, table cells)</li>
                        <li>Gap between repeating elements (ie button groups)</li>
                        <li>Padding within input components</li>
                        <li>Vertical spacing between elements in a card (ie a title and description, a description and actions)</li>
                        <li>Gap between the trigger and elevated element (ie between dropdown button & menu)</li>
                    </ul>
