## Characteristics

Primus Design System uses typography for 6 types of principles;

### Icon Clarity

An icon is a visual representation that can symbolize various elements such as objects, actions, sounds, gestures, states, or emotions. Its design should be minimalistic, consisting of basic shapes.

The purpose of an icon is for users to decode its metaphorical meaning, the implied message it conveys, without the reliance on text labels. Symbols like an apple, a phone, or pencils are universally recognized, eliminating the need for additional captions. The familiarity of icons is what makes them functional, as they can be easily understood and interpreted by users.

### Platform-specific

The Primus Design System aims for consistent icons across multiple platforms. While most icons remain similar across platforms, some may vary based on platform-specific conventions. For example, the "share" icon may have a platform-specific design to align with the respective platform's standards.

### Appearance

The core principle guiding the appearance of icons in the Primus Design System is simplicity. Each icon is designed to be as minimalistic as necessary, with unnecessary details omitted. The focus is on creating icons that effectively serve their specific purpose and function.

Another important aspect of the Primus Design System's icons is their color scheme. The icons follow a monochromatic approach, meaning they are typically rendered in a single color. The specific color of an icon may vary based on factors such as the interactive state (default, hover, disabled) or the chosen color theme within the design system. This approach ensures visual consistency and coherence across the entire icon set.

### Cultural Context

Understanding cultural perspectives on icons is vital. Different interpretations among people from diverse cultures can lead to misunderstandings or offense. Factors like language, location, and currency contribute to this variation.

### Density

Lines and or shapes must be 2px apart. The spacing of the elements used in the icons can be made 1px if needed. This spacing cannot be less than 1px. In cases where the gap is less than 1 px, the density of the shapes should be checked by eye and should be compatible with the general style.

### Proximity

When objects are close together, our eyes see them as a group. If they are at the same spot, draw them together. If they're not at the same spot, we use the figure-ground principle. This principle means we naturally see objects as either in the foreground (the main focus) or in the background. They appear as though they are in front of each other.
